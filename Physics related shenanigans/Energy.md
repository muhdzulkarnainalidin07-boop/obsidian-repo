### Work–kinetic energy theorem
Work–KE theorem
W_net = ΔK = K_f − K_i
Net work done = change in kinetic energy. Always.
### Potential energy types
Gravitational PE
U_g = mgh
Reference point (h=0) is your choice

Spring PE
U_s = ½kx²
x = compression/extension from rest
### Conservation of mechanical energy
No friction
ΔE_mec = ΔK + ΔU = 0
Total mechanical energy stays constant

With friction
W = ΔE_mec + f_k·d
Friction converts mechanical energy to thermal energy

f_k·d = thermal energy generated by friction (always positive — it's energy lost, not work done against direction).

### Finding force from potential energy
Force from PE curve
F(x) = −dU/dx
Force is the negative slope of the PE curve

Equilibrium = where dU/dx = 0. Turning point = where K = 0 (all energy is PE).

### Power
Average power
P_avg = W/t

Instantaneous power
P = dW/dt = F·v
