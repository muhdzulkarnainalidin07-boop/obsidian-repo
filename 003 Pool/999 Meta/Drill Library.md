# Drill Library

> Reference drills to pull from when planning sessions. Rate yourself 1–10 honestly.
> A drill you can do at 9/10 needs to be made harder — smaller zones, longer distance, pressure situations.

---

## CB control drills

### Stop shot line
Place CB and OB on the same line at various distances (1–5 feet). Pocket the OB and stop the CB dead. Track how many out of 10 you can stop within 3 inches of contact point.
**Progression:** increase distance → mix distances randomly → add a target zone for CB to stop in.

### Draw distance control
Set a specific draw target 1, 2, and 3 feet behind the contact point. Pocket OB, draw to target zone. 10 attempts per distance.
**What to watch:** is your miss always in the same direction? Usually a stroke delivery issue.

### Follow zones
Define 3 zones past the OB (close, mid, far). Call your zone before each shot. 10 attempts, score your accuracy.
**Progression:** make zones smaller (half size).

### Speed ladder
Shoot the same shot 5 times with 5 different speeds (feather to full) and observe CB path. No scoring — this is feel training.

## Position drills

### L-drill
9-ball style. Set up a simple 3–4 ball sequence. The only metric: did CB end up in the zone you planned? Score position, not just pockets.

### One-ball position loop
Single OB, single pocket. Shoot it, get position for the same shot again. Run as many in a row as possible. Cap at 25 attempts. Record personal best.

### Ghost ball (full rack)
Break, then run the rack as if you're playing 9-ball. No opponent. Track: balls pocketed before first miss, and whether you left yourself in position or forced shots.

## Break drills

### CB placement
10 breaks from your standard position. Mark where CB ends up each time. Goal: keep it within the center third of the table.

### Ball count
10 breaks. Record how many balls pocket each break. Anything below 1 consistently = power or contact issue.

### CB zone + ball count
Combine both. This is your real break metric.

## Pre-shot routine drill

### Routine timer
Practice your full PSR on a simple shot. Time it. Then use that as your consistent cadence on all practice shots for the session. Goal: same cadence whether the shot is easy or hard.

### Pressure PSR
Simulate pressure: if you don't complete your PSR before shooting, the rep doesn't count. Track how often you rush it.

---

## Safety drills

### Two-way shot
Set up a shot where you can either attack or play safe. Practice both from the same position. Score: did your safety actually leave them bad?

### Kick safety
OB behind the rack position. CB anywhere. Kick to OB and try to hide CB behind a rail or cluster. 10 attempts.

---

## Notes on making drills harder

The biggest mistake in pool practice is repeating drills you've already mastered at comfortable distances and angles. When you hit 8/10 consistently:
- Move the OB further
- Reduce the target zone size
- Add a required CB landing zone
- Do it in a sequence (position required for a follow-up shot)
- Add a consequence (missed position = restart the set)
