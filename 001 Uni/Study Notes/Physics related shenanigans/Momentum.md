### Momentum
Linear momentum
p = mv

Newton's 2nd law: F_net = dp/dt
If F_net = 0, then p = constant → conservation of momentum.
### Impulse
The total "push" over time — equals the change in momentum.

Impulse
J = Δp = F_avg · Δt
Also J = ∫F dt (area under F-t graph)

Short, massive force (like a bat hitting a ball) → same impulse as a long, gentle force — momentum change is equal.
### Collisions
Elastic collision
p_f = p_i AND K_f = K_i
Both momentum AND kinetic energy conserved. (Ideal — e.g. billiard balls)

Inelastic collision
p_f = p_i BUT K_f < K_i
Momentum conserved, KE lost to heat/sound. (Real world)
### Elastic collision shortcuts (when target at rest, v₂ᵢ = 0)
v₁ after
v₁f = (m₁−m₂)/(m₁+m₂) · v₁ᵢ
v₂ after
v₂f = 2m₁/(m₁+m₂) · v₁ᵢ
Equal masses (m₁=m₂): v₁f = 0, v₂f = v₁ᵢ — the first ball stops dead. Classic pool shot.
