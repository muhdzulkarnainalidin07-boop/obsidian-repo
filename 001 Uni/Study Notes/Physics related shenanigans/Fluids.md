### Pressure in a fluid
Pressure
p = F/A

Pressure with depth
p = p₀ + ρgh
Pressure increases linearly with depth
1 atm = 1.01 × 10⁵ Pa. Every 10 m of water adds about 1 atm.
### Pascal's principle
Pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted equally everywhere.
Hydraulic system
F_i/A_i = F_o/A_o
Small force on small piston → large force on big piston. (Hydraulic jacks, brakes)
Work in = Work out. You gain force but lose distance.
### Archimedes' principle
Buoyant force
F_b = ρ_fluid · V_submerged · g
Equal to the weight of fluid displaced

Object floats if ρ_object < ρ_fluid. Steel ships float because of their hollow shape — average density is less than water.
### Fluid flow
Continuity equation
A₁v₁ = A₂v₂
Narrower pipe → faster flow (same volume per second)

Bernoulli's equation
p + ½ρv² + ρgy = constant
Faster flow → lower pressure

Bernoulli explains airplane lift, carburettors, and why shower curtains blow inward.
