### Newton's three laws
1st law (inertia)
If F_net = 0, v = constant
Objects stay in their current state unless pushed

2nd law
F_net = ma
Net force = mass × acceleration

3rd law
F₁₂ = −F₂₁
Every force has an equal and opposite reaction
### Friction
Static friction
f_s ≤ μ_s · F_N
Resists motion before sliding starts. Adjusts to match applied force.

Kinetic friction
f_k = μ_k · F_N
Once sliding, this is fixed. μ_k < μ_s always.
μ_s > μ_k because static surfaces have tighter contact — once sliding, there's a slight gap between surfaces.
### Drag and terminal velocity
Drag force
D = ½CρAv²
C = drag coefficient, ρ = air density, A = cross-section area

Terminal velocity
v_t = √(2mg/CρA)
When drag = weight → no more acceleration
Skydivers reach ~200 km/h tucked, ~120 km/h spread-eagle — bigger area = more drag = slower terminal velocity.
### Centripetal force
Centripetal force
F_c = mv²/r
Not a new force — it's whatever force is pointing toward the centre (tension, normal, gravity, friction…)
At the top of a loop: gravity + normal force provide centripetal force. Minimum speed means normal force = 0, so mg = mv²/r → v = √(gR).
