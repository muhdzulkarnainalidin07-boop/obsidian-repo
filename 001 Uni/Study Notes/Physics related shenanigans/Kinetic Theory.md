### Ideal gas law
Gas pressure, volume, and temperature are linked. For an ideal gas (no real intermolecular forces):

Using moles
pV = nRT
R = 8.31 J/mol·K

Using molecules
pV = NkT
k = R/N_A = Boltzmann constant
### Work done by gas
General
W = ∫ p dV

Isothermal (const T)
W = nRT ln(V_f/V_i)
Temperature constant, volume changes

Isobaric (const p)
W = p(V_f − V_i)

Isochoric (const V)
W = 0
No volume change → no work done
### Internal energy and specific heats
Internal energy (monoatomic)
E_int = (3/2)nRT

Const volume heat
C_V = (3/2)R = fR/2
f = degrees of freedom (3 mono, 5 diatomic)

Const pressure heat
C_p = C_V + R

KE per molecule
K_avg = (3/2)kT
Temperature is literally average KE

Temperature = average kinetic energy of molecules. Hotter gas = faster molecules.
### RMS speed
Root mean square speed
v_rms = √(3RT/M)
M = molar mass. Lighter gases move faster at same temperature.

Helium balloons deflate faster than air-filled ones — He atoms move faster and escape more easily.
### Adiabatic process

No heat exchange (Q = 0). The gas is thermally insulated.

Pressure-volume
p_i V_i^γ = p_f V_f^γ

Temperature-volume
T_i V_i^(γ−1) = T_f V_f^(γ−1)

Gamma (γ)
γ = C_p/C_V
≈ 1.67 monoatomic, ≈ 1.4 diatomic
