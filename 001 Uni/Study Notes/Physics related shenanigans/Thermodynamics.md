### First law of thermodynamics
First law
ΔE_int = Q − W
Q = heat absorbed by gas. W = work done BY the gas. ΔE_int = change in internal energy.
Energy can't be created or destroyed — just moved around as heat or work.

### Special process cases
Adiabatic (Q = 0)
ΔE_int = −W
Gas does work → internal energy drops → cools

Isochoric (W = 0)
ΔE_int = Q
All heat goes into internal energy

Cyclical (closed loop)
Q = W
ΔE_int = 0 over a full cycle

Free expansion
Q = 0, W = 0
T_i = T_f (gas expands into vacuum)
### Temperature scales
Kelvin ↔ Celsius
T_C = T_K − 273.15

Celsius ↔ Fahrenheit
T_F = (9/5)T_C + 32

***Always use Kelvin in gas law equations. Never plug in Celsius directly.**

### Heat and phase changes
Heat with temp change
Q = mcΔT
c = specific heat capacity

Phase change
Q = Lm
L = latent heat. No temperature change during phase change.

Boiling water: temperature stays at 100°C until ALL water is vaporised — that energy goes into the phase change, not temperature.
