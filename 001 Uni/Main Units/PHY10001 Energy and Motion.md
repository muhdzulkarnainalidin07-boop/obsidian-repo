1. ==[[Simple Harmonic Motion]]==
Simple Harmonic Motion — any oscillating system where the restoring force is proportional to displacement. Springs, pendulums, tuning forks all do this.
2. ==[[Transverse Waves]]==
A wave where the disturbance is **perpendicular** to the direction the wave travels. Think of shaking a rope side-to-side — the rope moves up and down, but the wave travels forward.
3. ==[[Rotational Motion]]==
 **Rotational motion** is the movement of an object spinning around a fixed internal or external axis. Unlike linear motion (where an object moves from one point to another in a straight line), rotational motion involves objects staying in one general location while turning
4. ==[[Kinetic Theory]]==
The kinetic theory explains the macroscopic properties of matter (such as pressure, volume, temperature, and viscosity) by modeling it as countless atoms and molecules in constant, random motion. It bridges the gap between microscopic particle behavior and measurable thermodynamics
5. ==[[Thermodynamics]]==
**Thermodynamics** is the branch of physics and chemistry that studies the relationships between heat, work, temperature, and energy. It describes how energy is transferred from one form to another and how it affects the physical properties of matter.
6. ==[[Momentum]]==
Momentum is the strength or force an object has when moving, physically defined as mass in motion. It measures how difficult it is to stop a moving object.
7. ==[[Energy]]==
At its core, **energy** is the capacity to do work or produce change. It is a fundamental property of the universe that cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed from one form to another.
8. ==[[Kinematics]]==
**Kinematics** is a branch of physics and classical mechanics that describes the motion of objects (points, bodies, and systems of bodies) without considering the forces that cause the motion. It is often referred to as the "geometry of motion".
9. ==[[Forces]]==
A force is an action that changes or maintains the motion of an object, or causes it to deform. It is a vector quantity, meaning it has both magnitude (strength) and direction.
10. ==[[Fluids]]==
A **fluid** is any substance that flows continuously, deforms under external pressure, and takes the shape of its container. This includes all **liquids** and **gases**.
